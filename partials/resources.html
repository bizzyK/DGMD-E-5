Resources

Tools to help schools, families, and communities engage with the Connecticut River.

The Documentary

The Connecticut River Documentary Website – Explore the origins of the film project and its foundational vision through archived content and footage.

Learning Tools & Activities

  • EnviroScape® Watershed Model – Hands-on learning tool available to schools for teaching watershed dynamics and pollution.
  • Water Way(s) Curriculum – Interdisciplinary art and science activities for exploring river themes.
  • Migratory Fish Education – Learn about local species like eels and alewives through classroom materials and fish counting events.
  • Mill River Exploration – Field-based learning focused on ecology and river stewardship.
  • Connecticut River Paddlers’ Trail – Maps and guides for canoeing and exploring the river system.
  • Aquatic Invasive Species Management – Volunteer-based education about biodiversity and invasive plant removal.
  • Earth Day Initiatives – Community engagement through cleanups and educational programs.
